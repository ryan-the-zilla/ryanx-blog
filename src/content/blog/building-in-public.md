The default in tech is to build in secret. Work on your project for months, launch with a bang, and hope people care. Most of the time, they do not.

There is a better way.

## What Building in Public Means

Sharing your process. Your wins, your failures, your decisions, your reasoning. In real-time. Not as a polished case study after the fact.

## Why It Works

**Audience before product.** By the time you launch, people already care. They have been following your journey. They are invested.

**Feedback loop.** You get feedback while building, not after. Course corrections cost nothing when you make them early.

**Accountability.** When people are watching, you ship. Public commitments are the most powerful motivator.

**Trust.** People trust people who are transparent. They can see your reasoning, your trade-offs, your mistakes. That builds more credibility than any marketing page.

## How to Do It

1. **Share decisions, not just outcomes.** "I chose Astro over Next.js because..." is more valuable than "I launched my blog."
2. **Be honest about failures.** "This approach did not work because..." is more interesting than "everything is going great."
3. **Show your work.** Screenshots, code snippets, architecture diagrams. Let people see the process.
4. **Be consistent.** One post is a spike. Regular updates are a signal.

## The Fear

People worry about looking stupid. About competitors copying them. About being judged.

Here is the truth: nobody is watching as closely as you think. And the people who are watching? They are the ones who will become your users, your supporters, your community.
