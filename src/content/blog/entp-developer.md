If you are an ENTP developer, you already know the struggle. You start three projects before finishing one. You see possibilities everywhere. You argue with your own code.

But that chaos? It is your superpower. You just need to learn how to wield it.

## The Strengths

**Pattern recognition on steroids.** ENTPs see connections that others miss. You look at a problem and instantly see five different approaches. Most developers see one.

**Argument as a tool.** You do not argue to win. You argue to understand. This makes you exceptional at code reviews and system design discussions.

**Rapid prototyping.** You go from idea to working prototype faster than anyone. Your brain does not need a detailed plan — it needs a problem to solve.

## The Pitfalls

**Shiny object syndrome.** New framework? New tool? New language? You are already reading the docs while your current project gathers dust.

**Completion resistance.** Starting is exciting. Finishing is boring. The last 10% of a project feels like pulling teeth.

**Over-engineering.** You see all the ways something could be built. So you build all of them. Then none of them ship.

## How to Ship Anyway

1. **One project at a time.** Seriously. Pick one. Ignore the rest.
2. **Time-box exploration.** Give yourself 30 minutes to research something new. Then decide: commit or drop it.
3. **Ship ugly.** A finished mediocre project beats an abandoned perfect one.
4. **Find an accountability partner.** Someone who will call you out when you start something new.

## The Bottom Line

Your brain is wired for innovation, not maintenance. Accept that. Build systems that compensate for your weaknesses, and lean hard into your strengths.

The tech world needs more people who see what others cannot. Just make sure you actually finish what you start.
